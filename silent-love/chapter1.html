The Girl In The Rain

Some people arrive silently and change your entire world.

"Some love stories begin with words. Ours began with silence."

Rain touched the city softly that evening. Not the loud kind of rain people run away from... but the quiet kind that makes lonely people feel understood.

The entire college campus looked blurred behind silver droplets sliding endlessly through cold evening air. Students rushed through corridors protecting bags above their heads, laughing loudly, complaining about assignments, trying to survive another ordinary day.

But for Arjun... nothing felt ordinary anymore.

He sat beside the large library window, holding a book he hadn’t actually read for the past thirty minutes. His fingers rested between pages, yet his eyes stayed somewhere else entirely.

Three tables away from him.

She sat quietly near the rain-covered glass wall, softly flipping pages of a novel she probably wasn’t reading either.

Her name was Mira.

She wore a simple white kurti slightly darkened by rainwater near the sleeves. Tiny droplets rested on her hair like crystals reflecting library lights. Every few minutes, she tucked loose strands behind her ear while staring outside the window as if searching for something only she could see.

Arjun looked away immediately whenever she moved. Not because he didn’t want to see her... but because he was scared she might notice him staring.

There are people who enter life loudly. People who demand attention. People who force the world to notice them.

Mira was different.

She entered rooms quietly... yet somehow changed the atmosphere completely.

And maybe that silence is what connected them. Because Arjun was silent too.

"The quietest hearts often carry the loudest storms."

Outside, he looked normal. He laughed with friends. Submitted assignments. Attended lectures. Pretended life was stable.

But inside? Inside he carried loneliness so old it had become part of him.

He noticed tiny details about people. Especially sadness.

And Mira carried sadness beautifully.

Not dramatically. Not visibly. But hidden carefully behind tired smiles and calm eyes.

Sometimes she stared outside windows for too long. Sometimes she smiled at jokes half a second late. Sometimes her eyes looked distant even when surrounded by people.

Arjun noticed everything.

And slowly... without conversations, without friendship, without permission... love began growing silently inside him.

Days passed. Then weeks.

He started arriving at the library earlier just to sit near her usual table. Not too close. Not too far. Just enough to exist inside the same silence.

Sometimes she drank coffee while reading. Sometimes she wrote random lines in a small black notebook. Sometimes she simply stared at rain for several minutes without moving.

And every single time... Arjun wondered what kind of pain creates eyes like hers.

One evening, the electricity suddenly went off inside the library. Darkness covered the room. Students complained loudly. Phone flashlights appeared everywhere.

But Arjun noticed only one thing.

Mira looked uncomfortable. Not dramatically afraid. Just quietly uneasy... as if darkness reminded her of memories she didn’t want to revisit.

Without thinking, Arjun walked near her table and placed his small emergency lamp beside her books.

He didn’t say anything. He simply left it there and started walking away.

"Sometimes love begins in the smallest acts nobody else notices."

Then he heard it.

“Thank you.”

The first word she had ever spoken to him.

Arjun stopped walking.

For a second, the noise of rain disappeared completely.

He turned slightly.

She smiled softly. Not a dramatic smile. Not a fake polite smile. A real one. The kind that stays inside your chest forever.

That night, Arjun couldn’t sleep.

Not because something extraordinary happened. But because sometimes, one small moment becomes enough to change an entire life.

He replayed her voice repeatedly inside his mind. Soft. Gentle. Tired. Beautiful.

Outside, rain continued touching the city through midnight silence. Inside, a silent love had finally taken its first breath.

"And somewhere between rain, books, and silence... two lonely hearts had already started finding each other."
