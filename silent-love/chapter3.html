Under One Umbrella

Some distances disappear in silence before words can even try.

"And suddenly... the silence between them no longer felt empty."

Rain poured heavily outside the library building. Streetlights reflected beautifully across wet roads while cold wind pushed tiny droplets beneath the roof where students waited impatiently for the storm to calm down.

But Arjun noticed only one thing.

Mira stood near the edge of the entrance, holding her bag tightly while staring at the rain with visible worry hidden behind calm eyes.

She searched inside her bag once again. Then stopped.

No umbrella.

Arjun’s heartbeat slowly became louder inside his chest.

His mind started fighting itself immediately.

Should he help? Would she feel uncomfortable? What if she thinks he’s weird? What if she says no?

But sometimes feelings move faster than fear.

And before he could overthink again, his feet had already started walking toward her.

“Mira...”

The first time he had ever spoken her name aloud.

She turned slowly.

For a brief second, surprise appeared in her eyes. Then came that same soft smile he had already started memorizing unconsciously.

“Yes?”

Her voice sounded gentle even beneath the loud rain.

Arjun swallowed nervously before lifting the umbrella slightly.

“You can... come with me if you want.”

A small sentence. Simple. Awkward. Honest.

The kind of sentence that sounds ordinary to everyone else... but means everything to someone who spent days building courage silently.

"Sometimes bravery is nothing more than speaking despite fear."

Mira looked outside again. The rain had only become stronger.

Then she looked back at him.

“Are you sure?”

Arjun nodded immediately. Too quickly.

She noticed. And smiled again.

“Okay.”

That single word felt strangely dangerous to his heart.

Together, they stepped into the rain beneath one umbrella.

The world outside felt colder than before. Rain tapped softly against the umbrella above them while distant thunder echoed somewhere across the dark evening sky.

They walked slowly beside each other through the empty campus road.

Neither knew what to say.

But strangely... the silence didn’t feel awkward anymore.

It felt peaceful.

Mira walked carefully near puddles while holding the edge of her sleeves slightly above the water. Cold wind occasionally pushed rain beneath the umbrella, forcing them closer for brief accidental moments.

Each tiny moment completely destroyed Arjun’s ability to think normally.

Her perfume mixed softly with the smell of rain.

And somehow... that became his favorite scent instantly.

“So...” Mira spoke softly while looking ahead. “You always stay in the library that late?”

Arjun looked slightly surprised.

She was trying to continue the conversation.

“No... not always.”

“Only when it rains?”

A tiny smile appeared on her face.

Arjun laughed quietly.

“Maybe.”

"Sometimes the smallest conversations become unforgettable simply because of who they happen with."

The rain slowly became softer as they reached the quieter side of campus near old trees and empty benches.

Streetlights reflected across wet pathways beautifully. Everything around them felt strangely cinematic, like the world itself had slowed down just for this moment.

Mira suddenly stopped walking.

Arjun stopped too.

She looked at a small roadside tea stall glowing warmly beneath yellow lights. Steam rose softly into cold evening air.

“I love tea when it rains,” she said quietly.

Arjun stared at the shop for a second. Then back at her.

“Me too.”

Neither of them moved immediately.

Then Mira smiled softly.

“Let’s go?”

Arjun genuinely thought his heart might stop working.

The tea stall smelled like ginger, rainwater, and warm conversations. Old songs played quietly from a tiny radio near the counter while steam fogged the surrounding glass containers.

They sat near the corner beneath dim yellow light.

For the first time, they were truly sitting together.

Not as strangers sharing silence inside a library.

But as two people slowly beginning to know each other.

Mira wrapped both hands around the warm tea cup while staring at the rain outside.

“You like rain a lot, don’t you?” Arjun asked carefully.

She stayed silent for a few seconds before answering.

“Rain makes everything quieter.”

Her answer sounded simple. But something about it felt deeply personal.

Arjun looked at her silently.

Then Mira looked outside again and spoke softly.

“When it rains... people stop pretending for a while.”

That sentence stayed inside him immediately.

"Some people speak softly because their thoughts are louder than everyone else’s."

Outside, rain continued touching the city gently through the night.

And somewhere between warm tea, shared silence, and one small umbrella... two lonely hearts had unknowingly taken another step toward each other.
