Chapter 5

The Bench Near Block C

The Bench Near Block C

Some people quietly become part of your everyday life.

"He started missing her before she even left."

After that rainy evening, something between them changed quietly.

Not dramatically. Not suddenly.

But enough for both of them to start searching for each other unconsciously every single day.

Arjun started waking up earlier than usual.

And for the first time in years, mornings didn’t feel heavy anymore.

Because now, there was always something waiting for him at college.

Someone.

The library slowly became less about books and more about Mira.

Without planning it, they started sitting together almost every day.

Sometimes they talked. Sometimes they didn’t.

But strangely... silence around her never felt uncomfortable to Arjun anymore.

It felt peaceful.

Like resting after carrying loneliness for too long.

"Not every connection needs constant conversation. Some people understand each other quietly."

A new routine slowly formed between them.

Morning library. Tea after classes. Evening walks whenever it rained.

And somewhere between repeated little moments... they unknowingly started becoming important to each other.

One afternoon, Mira discovered an old bench near Block C behind the quieter side of campus.

Tall trees surrounded the area while rainwater flowed softly beside broken pathways. Almost nobody came there.

“I like this place,” she said quietly.

Arjun looked around.

Then at her.

And smiled softly.

“Me too.”

From that day onward, the bench near Block C slowly became theirs.

Some days they shared coffee there. Some days they sat silently watching rain. Some days Mira read random lines from novels aloud while Arjun listened quietly.

And every single day, Arjun fell a little deeper without realizing it.

One evening, cold wind moved softly through the trees while dark clouds covered the sky above campus.

Mira sat beside him quietly, holding a paper cup between both hands.

“You know something?” she asked suddenly.

“What?”

“I used to hate college.”

Arjun looked at her carefully.

“But now?” he asked softly.

Mira smiled slightly while staring ahead.

“Now it feels lighter somehow.”

"Sometimes one person changes the emotional weight of your entire world."

Arjun looked away immediately after hearing that. Because if he continued staring at her, he was scared his feelings might become visible.

For several seconds, only the sound of wind filled the silence around them.

Then suddenly, another voice interrupted.

“Mira?”

Both turned together.

A tall boy from another department walked toward them smiling confidently.

“Oh finally,” he laughed. “I’ve been searching everywhere for you.”

Mira smiled politely.

“Sorry, Aarav. I forgot.”

Arjun stayed silent.

But something inside him tightened unexpectedly.

The boy sat casually beside Mira and continued talking comfortably while Arjun listened quietly.

And for the first time since meeting her... he felt something unfamiliar.

Jealousy.

Not anger. Not irritation.

Just a strange uncomfortable heaviness he didn’t know how to explain.

Suddenly, he became aware of every little thing.

How easily Aarav made her laugh. How naturally they talked. How comfortable he seemed around her.

And for the first time, Arjun realized something terrifying.

Mira was becoming emotionally important to him. Far more than he had admitted to himself.

"The scariest part of falling for someone is realizing they can hurt you without even trying."

After a while, Aarav finally left.

Silence returned between them.

But this time, it felt different.

Heavier.

Mira noticed immediately.

“You became quiet suddenly,” she said softly.

Arjun forced a small smile.

“Just tired.”

She stared at him for a few seconds like she knew he was lying. But didn’t push further.

The evening slowly became darker while cold wind moved through the trees around them.

Then unexpectedly, Mira spoke again.

“You know what’s strange?”

Arjun looked at her.

“What?”

She smiled softly. A little differently this time. Warmer. More personal.

“You feel strangely familiar.”

That sentence completely destroyed whatever emotional distance he still had left.

Because deep inside, Arjun had already started feeling the exact same thing.

And somewhere beneath cloudy skies, old trees, and quiet evenings near Block C... two lonely hearts were slowly becoming each other’s favorite place.

"Some people enter your life quietly... and suddenly leaving them becomes unimaginable."
