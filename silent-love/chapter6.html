Midnight Conversations

Some people slowly become the first thought in your mornings and the last one before sleep.

"Attachment begins quietly... through repeated little moments your heart starts depending on."

After that evening near Block C, Arjun couldn’t stop thinking about Mira’s words.

“You feel strangely familiar.”

The sentence replayed endlessly inside his mind like an unfinished song.

Because deep inside... he had already started feeling the exact same thing.

The next few days passed differently.

Not because something dramatic happened. But because now... their connection felt real.

No longer accidental. No longer temporary.

Mira had quietly become part of his daily life.

And Arjun had unknowingly become part of hers too.

Morning library sessions. Tea near Block C. Shared silence during rain. Tiny smiles across crowded hallways.

The relationship still had no name.

Yet somehow... it already felt important.

"Sometimes the deepest relationships begin long before anyone confesses love."

One evening, while Arjun sat alone in his room pretending to study, his phone suddenly vibrated softly beside him.

Unknown Number.

His eyebrows narrowed slightly.

Then another message appeared.

“Mira here. Aarav gave me your number :)”

Arjun genuinely forgot how to breathe for a second.

He stared at the message for almost thirty seconds before replying.

“Hi.”

Then immediately deleted it.

Too dry.

He typed again.

“Hey.”

Deleted again.

Too eager.

Finally after overthinking for two entire minutes, he sent:

“Hey Mira :)”

And somehow, that tiny message made his heart race harder than exams ever had.

Her reply came almost immediately.

“You overthink texts a lot, don’t you?”

Arjun froze.

“How did you know?”

“Because you took suspiciously long to type one word.”

He laughed quietly to himself.

And just like that... their first late-night conversation began.

"Sometimes closeness begins through tiny glowing screens during lonely nights."

Hours passed without either of them noticing.

They talked about random things at first.

Favorite songs. Rain. Books. Childhood memories. Embarrassing school stories.

But slowly... the conversations became deeper.

Mira confessed she hated loud places.

Arjun admitted he often felt emotionally disconnected from people around him.

And strangely, neither of them felt judged.

Only understood.

At 1:17 AM, Mira suddenly sent:

“Can I tell you something weird?”

Arjun smiled unconsciously while typing.

“Always.”

Three dots appeared. Then disappeared. Then appeared again.

Finally:

“I feel calmer when I talk to you.”

"Certain messages become permanent memories the moment you read them."

Arjun stared at the screen silently.

Something inside him softened painfully.

Because nobody had ever said something like that to him before.

And maybe that’s why Mira was becoming dangerous to his heart.

Not because she was perfect.

But because she understood parts of him nobody else ever noticed.

Outside, rain touched his bedroom window softly while the city slept beneath dim midnight lights.

Inside, their conversation continued endlessly.

At some point, Mira sent a blurry picture of rain outside her apartment balcony.

“Looks peaceful tonight.”

Arjun looked at the image for several seconds before replying:

“Yeah. It does.”

But honestly... he wasn’t looking at the rain.

He was looking at the feeling behind the message.

The comfort. The trust. The quiet intimacy forming between them slowly.

"Love often begins the moment someone becomes your favorite notification."

Night after night, the conversations continued.

And without realizing it, both of them started depending on those late-night talks emotionally.

Some nights they laughed endlessly.

Some nights they spoke about fears they never shared with anyone else.

Some nights they simply stayed online together in silence.

Yet even silence felt comforting now.

One night, around 2 AM, Mira suddenly asked:

“Do you ever get scared people will leave eventually?”

Arjun’s fingers paused above the keyboard.

The question felt too real. Too personal.

After several seconds, he replied honestly:

“All the time.”

Mira didn’t respond immediately.

Then finally:

“Me too.”

At that moment, Arjun realized something terrifying.

He was no longer simply attracted to Mira.

He was emotionally attached to her now.

Her messages affected his mood. Her absence felt noticeable. Her happiness mattered to him more than it should.

And somewhere deep inside... his heart had already started building a future around someone who still had no idea how deeply she was loved.

"The scariest kind of love is the one that grows quietly before you even realize it’s happening."

Near 3 AM, Mira finally sent:

“You should sleep.”

Arjun smiled faintly.

“So should you.”

“Goodnight, Arjun.”

That simple sentence somehow felt warmer than everything else in the room.

He typed slowly:

“Goodnight, Mira.”

Then stayed awake for another hour staring at the conversation after she went offline.

Because sometimes... happiness arrives quietly through midnight messages and rainy nights.

And sometimes... that happiness becomes impossible to live without.

"Some people slowly become home before you even realize your heart has moved in."
