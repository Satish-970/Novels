The Fear of Losing You

Sometimes love becomes real the moment fear enters it.

"The heart gets attached quietly... then suddenly becomes terrified of losing the person."

After that evening near Block C, something changed inside Arjun completely.

Mira’s words stayed with him constantly.

“I think you’re the only person who actually sees me.”

No one had ever trusted him emotionally like that before.

And maybe that’s why her sadness affected him so deeply now.

The next few days felt softer somehow.

Mira smiled more often. She laughed more freely. And occasionally, for very brief moments, the heaviness inside her eyes disappeared completely.

Arjun noticed every tiny change.

Because when you truly love someone, you begin memorizing even the smallest versions of their happiness.

"Love is noticing details nobody else pays attention to."

One rainy evening, they sat together inside the nearly empty library while thunder echoed softly outside.

Mira rested her chin against her hand while reading quietly.

Arjun pretended to focus on his book. But honestly... he had been staring at the same page for ten minutes.

Because every time he looked at her, his chest felt painfully warm.

And it was becoming harder to hide.

“Why do you keep looking at me?”

Arjun froze instantly.

Mira smiled without lifting her eyes from the book.

“You do it a lot.”

His heartbeat nearly stopped.

“I wasn’t staring.”

“You were.”

A small laugh escaped her lips softly.

Arjun looked away immediately, completely embarrassed.

But strangely... Mira didn’t seem uncomfortable.

If anything, she looked amused. Comfortable. Maybe even happy.

That realization terrified him more than rejection ever could.

Because now, his feelings were becoming visible.

"The hardest part of love is pretending your heart isn’t revealing everything already."

Later that night, their usual midnight conversation continued again.

But tonight felt different somehow.

More vulnerable. More dangerous.

At 1:42 AM, Mira suddenly sent:

“Can I ask something personal?”

Arjun stared at the message for several seconds before replying.

“Yeah.”

Three dots appeared slowly.

Then:

“Why are you so kind to me?”

His chest tightened painfully.

Because the real answer was impossible to say.

Because I love you.

Because your sadness feels personal to me now.

Because my entire day revolves around your existence.

Because losing you already scares me.

But instead, he typed:

“Because you matter to me.”

Mira read the message immediately.

But didn’t reply for almost two minutes.

Then finally:

“You matter to me too.”

Arjun closed his eyes slowly after reading it.

Because some sentences feel dangerous when they’re exactly what your heart desperately wanted.

"Sometimes the smallest confessions carry the deepest meanings."

Outside, rain touched his window softly while the city slept beneath silver midnight lights.

Inside, his feelings were becoming impossible to control anymore.

And maybe Mira felt it too.

Because lately, the way she looked at him had changed slightly.

Longer eye contact. Softer smiles. Comfortable silence.

Like her heart was slowly moving closer too.

One afternoon, while they walked together across campus beneath cloudy skies, Mira suddenly stopped near the old staircase beside Block B.

“What?” Arjun asked softly.

She looked strangely nervous for a second.

Then quietly:

“I’m scared.”

“Of what?”

Mira looked away toward the rain-covered campus roads.

“Getting too attached.”

Arjun’s heartbeat slowed painfully.

Because deep down, he knew exactly what she meant.

When people become emotionally important, fear follows naturally.

Fear of losing them. Fear of distance. Fear of becoming memories.

And maybe both of them had already started realizing this connection was becoming deeper than friendship.

"Love becomes frightening when the thought of losing someone starts hurting before they’re even gone."

Cold wind moved softly through Mira’s hair while rain continued falling quietly around campus.

Then suddenly, without thinking, Arjun spoke honestly.

“You don’t have to be scared with me.”

The moment the words left his mouth, silence filled the space between them instantly.

Mira looked at him slowly.

And for the first time since meeting her... Arjun saw emotion visibly break through the calmness in her eyes.

Not sadness. Not fear.

Something softer. Something deeper.

She smiled faintly.

Then stepped slightly closer beneath the rain.

“So don’t disappear either.”

That sentence completely shattered whatever emotional control he still had left.

Because now, for the first time, it truly felt like Mira was holding onto him too.

And suddenly, the possibility of losing her became the scariest thing he had ever imagined.

"Some people quietly become the reason your heart learns fear and happiness at the same time."

As rain continued falling around them softly, Arjun realized something dangerous.

He was no longer simply falling in love.

He was already there.
