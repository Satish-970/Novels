Part 1 • Chapter 2 • The Library Window

The Library Window

Sometimes people become part of your life before you even realize it.

"He didn’t realize he was waiting for her... until she became the favorite part of his day."

Morning sunlight entered the college library softly through tall glass windows, painting long golden shadows across old wooden tables. The smell of wet books, coffee, and fresh rain still floated quietly through the silent room.

Arjun arrived earlier than usual that day. Not because he had assignments. Not because exams were close. And definitely not because he suddenly cared about studying at eight in the morning.

He arrived because somewhere deep inside him, a quiet hope had already formed.

Maybe she would come again.

He sat at the same table near the large library window. The same place from yesterday. The same angle where he could see the entrance without making it obvious.

Outside, students crossed campus carrying umbrellas under cloudy skies while cold wind moved fallen leaves across wet pathways.

Inside, the ticking wall clock sounded louder than usual.

9:03 AM.

She still hadn’t arrived.

Arjun opened a random book and pretended to read. But every few seconds, his eyes moved toward the entrance again.

Then finally... the glass door opened.

And there she was.

Mira stepped inside quietly, holding two books close to her chest. Her hair looked slightly messy from the morning wind. A few tiny rain droplets still rested near her sleeves.

"Sometimes hearts react before minds even understand what’s happening."

Arjun immediately looked down at his book, pretending he hadn’t noticed her.

But his heartbeat had already betrayed him.

Mira walked toward her usual table. Then paused for one brief second.

Her eyes moved toward him.

And she smiled softly.

Not fully. Not dramatically. Just enough to make his entire chest feel strangely warm.

Arjun stared at the same page for almost five minutes after that without understanding a single word.

Because now something had changed.

Yesterday, they were strangers.

Today, they were strangers who shared a smile.

And somehow... that felt dangerously important.

Hours passed slowly. Rain tapped gently against the windows while silence wrapped around the library like soft music.

Mira occasionally wrote something inside her small black notebook. Every time she finished writing, she stared outside for several seconds before continuing again.

Arjun couldn’t stop wondering what she wrote about.

Poetry? Memories? Someone she missed?

Or maybe... things she could never say out loud.

Because he understood that feeling.

"Lonely people often recognize loneliness inside each other without needing words."

Some emotions become too heavy for conversations. So people bury them inside notebooks, songs, and midnight thoughts instead.

Around noon, the library became more crowded. Students whispered across tables. Phone vibrations echoed occasionally. Pages turned endlessly.

Then suddenly, a strong cold wind entered through the slightly open window near Mira’s seat.

Loose papers flew across the floor.

One notebook slipped from her table and landed near Arjun’s chair.

He picked it up carefully.

For a brief second, their fingers touched while she took it back.

Such a small thing. Such a tiny moment.

Yet somehow, it stayed inside him like electricity.

“Thank you,” she whispered again.

This time, her voice sounded softer. More comfortable.

Arjun nodded quietly, trying very hard not to look nervous.

But Mira noticed anyway.

And surprisingly... she smiled again.

That smile ruined him completely.

Because there are certain people who don’t just enter your life. They slowly begin living inside your thoughts.

Even after she left the library that evening, her presence remained everywhere.

On the empty chair across the room. Inside unfinished silence. Between raindrops touching the glass window.

"Love rarely arrives dramatically. Sometimes it enters quietly through repeated little moments your heart refuses to forget."

Arjun stayed there long after sunset, pretending to study while secretly replaying every tiny interaction repeatedly inside his mind.

The smile. The voice. The accidental touch. The way her eyes looked tired and beautiful at the same time.

Outside, the rain slowly became heavier.

The entire city looked drowned in silver lights and wet roads while distant thunder echoed beyond dark clouds.

Arjun finally packed his bag and started walking toward the exit.

But near the library gate, he suddenly stopped.

Because standing beneath the roof outside... was Mira.

She looked worried.

She searched through her bag repeatedly while rain poured heavily only a few feet away.

Then Arjun realized.

She didn’t have an umbrella.

"And suddenly... the silence between them no longer felt empty."
