Midnight Letters

2:13 AM

Some absences feel louder than entire conversations.

Some absences hurt more than a hundred conversations ever could.

The third letter arrived three nights later.

And somehow... those three nights felt longer than entire months.

Aarush never realized how quickly people could become emotionally dependent on something invisible.

— Three nights of silence.

Every night after work, he returned to Apartment 306 with the same nervous heartbeat.

Same expectation. Same hope.

But the hallway remained empty.

No envelope. No handwriting. No sign of her.

And strangely... the absence affected him more than it should have.

Because now silence felt heavier after someone had interrupted it.

✦

The rain continued almost every night that week.

Cold water rolled endlessly across the apartment windows while distant city lights blurred beneath fog and thunder.

Aarush spent most evenings sitting near the window holding the previous letters carefully in his hands.

Reading them again. And again.

“You always stare at the rain before entering the building.”

“You look like someone waiting for life to begin again.”

Nobody had ever described him so accurately before.

Not even people who had known him for years.

And maybe that was the frightening part.

This unknown girl somehow understood emotions he never explained aloud.

Being known by a stranger is stranger than being a stranger to everyone you know.

At work, his behavior slowly changed too.

He checked his phone constantly. Lost focus during meetings. Stopped leaving immediately after office hours.

Sometimes he caught himself staring out the building windows while it rained outside.

Wondering if somewhere nearby... she existed quietly among strangers.

Maybe watching him again.

— Somewhere in the city, she existed.

That thought should have felt disturbing.

But honestly... it felt comforting now.

Because for the first time in years, someone noticed when he was hurting.

Someone cared enough to write.

✦

Three nights later, Aarush finally returned home around midnight after a particularly exhausting day.

Rainwater soaked his sleeves while thunder echoed softly across the city skyline.

The elevator moved slowly upward through the silent building.

Floor 2.
Floor 3.
Floor 4.

His heartbeat became nervous automatically.

Even though he told himself not to expect anything anymore.

The elevator doors opened.

Silent hallway. Flickering light. Cold midnight air.

And there it was.

Another envelope.

— Center of the apartment door.

This time placed directly in the center of his apartment door.

Aarush froze completely.

For several seconds, he simply stared at it while rain echoed faintly through the building outside.

Then slowly... he noticed something different.

The envelope looked rushed.

The handwriting slightly shaky.

And near the corner... a tiny rain stain blurred part of the ink.

Almost like whoever left it had been standing in the rain moments earlier.

The trembling in handwriting always reveals more than the words themselves.

A strange feeling moved through his chest.

For the first time... the unknown girl stopped feeling imaginary.

She felt real.

Human.

Close.

✦

Inside Apartment 306, he didn’t even remove his wet hoodie this time.

He sat down immediately beneath the kitchen light and opened the envelope carefully.

Another handwritten page.

But this letter felt more personal than the others.

Almost emotional.

“You looked sad tonight.”

“I almost didn’t leave this letter.”

“You looked like someone carrying too much alone.”

“Some nights your silence feels heavier than the rain.”

“I stood outside your door for almost five minutes tonight.”

“I wanted to ask if you were okay.”

“But strangers don’t ask questions like that.”

“Maybe lonely people recognize each other easily.”

Aarush’s breathing slowed instantly.

Because suddenly... this no longer sounded mysterious.

It sounded vulnerable.

Like the writer herself was struggling emotionally too.

She was there. Close enough to knock. Close enough to stay.

His chest tightened painfully.

Because somewhere deep inside... he suddenly realized something dangerous.

He wanted her to ask.

He wanted this unknown girl to stop being a mystery.

To knock. To stay. To exist somewhere beyond handwritten words.

2:13 AM

Just a time.

Nothing more.

But somehow... it felt intentional.

Like a hidden message.

Aarush immediately looked toward the digital clock near the kitchen.

12:41 AM

His heartbeat slowly became louder.

Why write the time?

Why 2:13 AM specifically?

✦

For the next hour, Aarush couldn’t focus on anything else.

Rain continued endlessly outside while the apartment filled with cold blue shadows from distant city lights.

1:26 AM
1:48 AM
2:02 AM

And suddenly... something inside him made a decision.

At exactly 2:10 AM, Aarush left Apartment 306 quietly and stepped into the empty hallway.

Cold air moved softly through the building.

The lights flickered faintly overhead.

Silence.

Complete silence.

Then...
2:13 AM

The elevator dinged softly at the far end of the hallway.

Aarush’s heartbeat stopped.

Slowly... the elevator doors began opening.

And someone stood inside.
