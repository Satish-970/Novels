Chapter II — Unknown Handwriting
CHAPTER 2

Unknown

Some sentences stay with you not because they're beautiful —
but because they're too accurate.

Unknown Handwriting

He barely slept. Even at 3 AM, the letter was still in his hand.

"Some sentences repeat inside you not because they're poetic — but because they're terrifyingly true."

Aarush barely slept after reading the first letter.

Even at 3 AM, he still sat near the apartment window holding the folded paper beneath dim blue city light.

"You looked tired again today."

The sentence repeated endlessly inside his mind.

Because no one had noticed him that carefully in years.

And honestly... that scared him more than the mystery itself.

— 3 AM. The city light. The letter still open.
✦

The next morning, he checked the hallway security camera before leaving for work.

Nothing.

No footage. No movement. No figure near Apartment 306.

Only empty hallways and flickering lights.

Somehow, that made everything stranger.

— Empty hallway. No footage. No figure. Nothing.
"The most unsettling mysteries leave no evidence behind. Only questions."

The entire day passed differently after that.

Aarush found himself distracted constantly.

Every unknown face made him wonder.

Every passing stranger suddenly felt suspicious.

Who was she? Why him? And how did she know things nobody else noticed?

At work, conversations echoed meaninglessly around him while coworkers laughed together near the cafeteria.

But Aarush remained distant. Not physically. Emotionally.

Like part of his mind still remained inside Apartment 306 beside a mysterious handwritten letter.

— Surrounded by people. Completely alone inside.
✦

Rain started again after sunset.

The city slowly disappeared beneath fog, headlights, and silver reflections across wet roads.

By 11:32 PM, Aarush stood inside the elevator returning home.

And this time... his heartbeat already felt nervous before the doors even opened.

Because part of him hoped another letter would be there.

That realization disturbed him immediately.

How could someone unknown already matter this much?

"You know you're in trouble when you start hoping for things you can't explain."

The elevator doors opened slowly.

Silent hallway. Flickering light. Cold air.

And there it was.

Another envelope.

Exactly near his apartment door. Same handwriting. Same black ink. Same strange familiarity.

— A second envelope. Same door. Same ink. Same feeling.

Aarush stopped walking instantly.

For several seconds, he simply stared at it while distant thunder echoed softly outside the building.

Then slowly, he looked around the hallway.

Nobody.

Only silence.

For the first time, fear mixed softly with curiosity inside his chest.

Because this no longer felt random.

Someone was watching carefully. Someone knew his routine.

And somehow... that person understood him emotionally already.

✦

Inside Apartment 306, rain touched the windows softly while distant thunder echoed across the city skyline.

Aarush sat down near the kitchen light again. Exactly like yesterday.

Except tonight, his loneliness felt different.

Less empty. More restless.

He opened the envelope carefully.

Another handwritten page. Another unknown piece of someone's heart.

— Dim kitchen light. Another letter unfolding.

"You pretend to be okay very convincingly."

"But sometimes your eyes look emotionally exhausted."

"You always sit beside the rain instead of turning on the television."

"You look like someone waiting for life to begin again."

"You looked happier tonight."

Aarush's breathing slowed after the first line.

The room suddenly felt colder.

Because once again... the letter sounded painfully accurate. Like the writer had seen parts of him nobody else ever noticed.

"Being understood by a stranger is more unsettling than being misunderstood by everyone you know."

The final line made him freeze completely.

"You looked happier tonight."

Because that meant she had seen him tonight too. Recently. Close enough to notice.

And suddenly, the apartment didn't feel empty anymore.

It felt watched.

Not in a threatening way. Something stranger than that.

Like someone cared quietly from the shadows.

And somehow... that felt dangerously comforting.

— Hundreds of windows. One unknown girl who knew his sadness.
✦

Aarush stood up slowly and walked toward the apartment window.

Rain covered the city endlessly outside.

Hundreds of glowing windows. Thousands of strangers.

Yet somewhere among them... one unknown girl knew his sadness better than anyone else.

And for the first time in years, he genuinely wanted tomorrow to arrive faster.

Because maybe another letter would come. Maybe another piece of her.

And maybe... without realizing it... his lonely heart had already started waiting for someone he had never even seen.

"Every story needs two people willing to begin. His had already started — he just didn't know hers had too."
